The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. 
It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower. 
Constructed from 1887 to 1889 as the entrance to the 1889 World's Fair, it is one of the most recognizable structures in the world.

The Amazon rainforest, also known as Amazonia, is a vast tropical rainforest in South America. 
It is the largest rainforest on Earth and represents over half of the planet's remaining rainforests. 
The majority of the forest is contained within Brazil, with significant portions in Peru, Colombia, and other countries.
